Fast Track vs Value Track
Start with a hypothesis. Confirm in discovery.
This is not a formula based on company size or site count. It is a working read you refine as you learn more. Use it to set your initial pacing, then adjust based on what discovery reveals.
Fast Track

Clear signal. Move now.

Your initial read suggests a shorter path. Test it early and move fast if discovery confirms it.

  • Clear trigger event visible from the outside (RTO mandate, new office, recent incident)
  • One or two personas likely own the problem without heavy committee involvement
  • Simpler entry point with a clean use case match
  • Discovery quickly surfaces urgency and ownership
  • Higher odds of near-term meeting and a short sales cycle
Adjust if: discovery reveals more stakeholders, a committee forming, or compliance requirements you did not expect. That shifts the motion toward Value Track.
Pull what SuccessAI has on [company] from my book, then classify it as Fast Track or Value Track. Give me the signals that support that read and exactly how it should shape my first outreach.
Value Track

Bigger upside. Play the long game.

Your initial read suggests a more complex motion. That is fine. It means more deal-shaping opportunity, not less.

  • Multi-site or complex global footprint with inconsistent workflows
  • Security, compliance, or regulatory sensitivity that raises the stakes
  • Multiple stakeholders with different priorities across functions
  • Transformation or standardization initiative already underway
  • Higher upside but requires earlier multi-threading and deal shaping
Adjust if: a single champion emerges quickly who can move fast and owns budget. That can open a faster lane even inside a complex account.
[company] looks like a Value Track account. Using any context SuccessAI has in my book, give me the sequencing strategy, who to involve and when, and the specific moves to shape the deal now.

How to use it well
Do this. Not this.
The difference between useful output and generic output is almost always in how you ask.
✅

Do this

  • Ask for a full account plan with context, not just "who should I target"
  • Include the account name and as much context as you have
  • Ask for stakeholder maps and let them guide your sequencing
  • Ask for persona-specific messaging when you know the title
  • Ask what to send first before you write anything
  • Ask for proof and case studies relevant to the persona
  • Push on the first output. Ask it to sharpen, shorten, or change the angle
  • Use Fast Track or Value Track as an initial hypothesis, then update it after discovery
  • Ask for discovery questions before a real call
  • Use it for meeting prep, not just prospecting
🚫

Not this

  • Do not ask for a cold email with no account context and expect a good result
  • Do not stop at research without turning it into an action
  • Do not blast every stakeholder at once with the same message
  • Do not assume the most senior title is the best cold entry point
  • Do not paste the output directly without checking it for context and fit
  • Do not skip the org chart step when you are breaking into a new account
  • Do not ask for a sequence if you do not yet know who you are targeting
  • Do not treat Fast Track vs Value Track as a fixed label. Revisit it after discovery
  • Do not ask vague questions and expect sharp output. Be specific
  • Do not use it once and move on. Refine the output before you send
FAQ
Common questions.
Quick answers so you can get moving.
What should I include in my prompt?+
At minimum: the company name, the contact name and title if you have them, whether the account is warm or cold, and what you are trying to accomplish. The more context you give, the sharper the output. You do not need to be perfect. Start with what you know.
What if I do not know who to target yet?+
Ask for the stakeholder map first. Give Claude-specting the account name and tell it what motion you are running. It will surface the relevant personas, rank them by best fit, and tell you where to start. The org chart is the right first step when you are starting from scratch.
What if the contacts are too senior to cold reach?+
Ask for the manager layer. Senior leaders at big companies are hard to reach cold. The managers underneath them own execution, respond more often, and can pull you up when the time is right. Ask Claude-specting to show you the manager layer under a specific VP or Director and it will map that for you.
What if I already have some account activity?+
Tell it. The more context you include about prior touches, prior conversations, and what has already been tried, the better the output. Claude-specting also pulls from SuccessAI for your Salesforce book of business, scoped to your own accounts, and checks Gmail, Google Drive, and Outreach when relevant, so it does not give you a cold plan for a warm account.
Can it pull from my own accounts?+
Yes. Claude-specting uses SuccessAI, Envoy's Salesforce connector, and scopes every account pull to your own book of business. You do not need to paste account names. Ask it things like "Using my Salesforce accounts in SuccessAI, which 5 should I prioritize this week and why." It only sees the accounts you own.
How do I make the output shorter or more punchy?+
Just ask. Add "make it shorter and more direct" or "cut this to under 80 words" and it will sharpen. You can also say "give me just the email, no explanation" if you do not need the rationale. Pushing on the first output is almost always worth it.
Should I ask for a sequence or just one email?+
If you know who you are targeting and you are ready to activate, ask for the full sequence. If you are still figuring out the angle or persona, start with one email and refine from there before building out. The sequence is most useful when you already have a clear point of view on the contact.
What if it gives me something that feels off?+
Push back on it. Tell it what feels wrong and ask it to adjust. You might say "the tone is too formal" or "this does not reflect what I know about this account" and it will rewrite. The first output is a starting point, not the final product.
